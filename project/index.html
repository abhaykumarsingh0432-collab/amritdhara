THE INDIAN WATER CRISIS

India is facing one of the world's most severe water crises, with millions of lives and livelihoods at risk. Over-extraction of groundwater, pollution of our rivers, and the unpredictable impacts of climate change are threatening our nation's future. The challenge is immense, but understanding the problem is the first step towards solving it.

Key Facts

600 Million Indians Face High Water Stress Demand May Exceed Supply by 2x by 2030 Nearly 70% of Our Water is Contaminated Agriculture Consumes ~80% of Freshwater

Simple Steps, Powerful Impact

At Home

Simple acts like fixing a dripping tap can save over 10,000 litres a year. Take 5-minute showers and turn off the tap while brushing to make a huge daily impact.

Indoors Daily Habits

In Your Garden

Water plants during the cooler hours of the day to reduce evaporation. Consider rainwater harvesting—a simple and effective way to conserve for outdoor use.
